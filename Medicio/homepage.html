    About Me

    I am currently a freshman at BYU Provo, and so far I have enjoyed my time here. I am currently studying to be an accounting major so that I can get an internship at one of the big four accounting firms. My favorite accounting field is auditing, but I am also a big fan of forensic accounting.

    Extracurriculars

    Here are some of the groups that I participate in at BYU:

    • I am a member of the BYU Accounting Society. This is a program that helps students find out what field of accounting they want to go into, as well as give ideas about how to build up your resume.
    • I am the Assistant Ward Financial Clerk of the 122nd Provo YSA ward. My main job is to take the receipts of purchases and put them into an online system. I also help people with tithing payments.
    • On Wednesday nights I play in the BYU Unniversity Orchestra. I play percussion and recently got to play in a concert where we performed famous pieces like Carmen and The Blue Danube Waltz.

    One thing that sets me apart from most other students is that I graduated with an Associates Degree in General Arts while I was still in high school. Because Minnesota education has a program where you can take classes through community colleges, I considered this idea as a way for me to get ahead in college credits. When the COVID-19 pandemic hit, I realized that my high school was unable to effectively transfer to a completely online format, so I made the decision to enroll in a community college. My next problem was when I found out that college credits are very hard to transfer unless if you have a degree. With that in mind, I then ended up getting an Associates Degree in General Arts so that the credits could more easily transfer over.

    Why did you choose BYU?

    When I was younger I was very interested in going to a unniversity that also focusses on church teachings. I also have a lot of family in Utah and Colorado, so that is an extra bonus for me. It wasn't until I started my application process that I found out that the Marriott School is one of the best business schools in the country.

    Why did you choose a degree in accounting?

    I took a few different classes to try and get an understanding of what I liked, but nothing really stuck. Programming with Python was fun but incredibly frustrating. Engineering was really easy for me, but not the most exciting. I choose accounting because it's fun for me to try and manage everything and to make sure all transactions are properly documented.

    Have you served a Mission yet

    Currently I have not served a full-time mission, but I plan on doing so after my freshman year at BYU. I just wanted to get an idea of what living on my own is going to be like before I leave.

    Where is your favorite place to travel to?

    My favorite place to travel is Spain. I know a small amount of spanish vocabulary, but I mainly love the culture. Everything over there is just more exciting and passionate compared to the US. Also the music and food are amazing (I hope this is where my Mission calls me to go, but I'll probably be happy with anything in Europe).

    Skills

    Here are a few of the skills that I have learned in my first semester at BYU:

    Basic Accounting Skills

    In my Accounting 200 class I learned about all the basics to GAAP (Generally Accepted Accounting Principles). I continue to learn more next semester in Accounting 320.

    Basic Python Programming

    One of the classes I took in order to get my Associates Degree was an introductory class to Python. This class has helped me out in more ways I could have imagined because it was an easier place for me to learn about basic programming concepts.

    Excel Spreadsheets

    Recently I finished IS 110, Intro to Spreadsheets and Analysis. This class taught me about how to use different functions and formulas in Excel so that I can more efficiently manage and solve problems.

    Tableau

    Tableau is a tool that lets you design your own charts and graphs. I was taught about how to use this in IS 201, and I even had to make my own chart for the IS 201 final about the different lengths of some of my favorite workout songs.

    HTML and CSS

    This was a little tricky for me to understand at first, but I think that I have a solid grasp on this now. Believe it or not, the hardest part web dev is trying to find software that will convert a JPEG file into a JPG file.

    Macros in Excel

    This allows you to program different calculations and functions into Excel spreadsheets. It's challenging work but also a lot of fun. I think that I was able to understand this easier than most because of the Python course I took.
